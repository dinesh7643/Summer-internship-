Natural Causes

One of the most significant natural sources of pollution are volcanoes, which during eruptions release large quantities of harmful gases into the

atmosphere. Volcanic gases include carbon dioxide, which can be fatal in large concentrations and contributes to Climate Change , hydrogen halides

which can cause acid rain, sulfur dioxides, which are harmful to animals and damage the Ozone Layer , and hydrogen sulfides, which are capable of

killing humans at concentrations of less than 1 part per thousand[19] Volcanic emissions also include fine and ultrafine particles which may contain

toxic chemicals and substances such as arsenic, lead, and mercury[20]
